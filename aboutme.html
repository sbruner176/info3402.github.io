Education University of Colorado Boulder BA in Molecular, Cellular, and Developmental Biology, Minor in Information Science Expected Graduation Date: December 2021 Research Projects Cell Biology Lab – Tetrahymena Gene Expression Research mentor: Alison Vigers, PhD Project title: The role of Beclin 1 in autophagy in Tetrahymena thermophila Many neurodegenerative diseases occur because of an autophagic malfunction. In this lab my group used Tetrahymena thermophila to look at genes involved in autophagy and apoptosis, specifically we looked at the gene encoding Beclin 1. To determine the activity of Beclin 1, several PCRs were run to test the Beclin 1 expression during different phases: starvation, log, and conjugation. We performed gel electrophoresis to look at the PCR results, which showed that Beclin 1 expression was high in all phases. This implied that even when the cell undergoes challenges Beclin 1 is equally as functional as when the cell is supplied with many nutrients and no stressors.
